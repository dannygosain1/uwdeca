Waterloo DECA Intrapreneurship Conference

On March 8th to 10th 2013, Waterloo DECA will be hosting our 8th annual business conference. Each year, the Waterloo DECA Conference attracts bright, business-minded students from top universities across Ontario and industry professionals. We invite our delegates to showcase their business talents, develop strategic intuition and build professional relationships. This year's theme is Intrapreneurship, a concept that illustrates the practice of entrepreneurship by employees within an organization. The University of Waterloo and its local region are renowned for innovation, and will challenge delegates to further that innovation in the realm of business.

Previous Conference Speakers

Steven Lightstone

President of Corner Office Leads


Steve Lightstone is one of the most experienced marketing executives for technology firms in the industry. He has experience managing top performing and high revenue growth teams, and has executed projects for IBM, SAP, and Microsoft. Steve founded Corner Office Leads, a marketing firm that helps sales organizations hunt for new leads at the senior executive level. He was also the Vice President of Sales & Marketing at Speechbobble, an up-and-coming social networking startup. Steve is looking forward to sharing his entrepreneurial experiences.

Gabriel Chan

Co-Founder of NoteWagon


Gabriel Chan is the Co-Founder of NoteWagon, a website that helps students get cash for their notes. The service has tens of thousands of users from universities all over North America. Now valued over $1 million, NoteWagon was featured in CBC's Dragons' Den, Mashable, and Huffington Post. Gabriel Chan is excited to provide his insights on iThink and tell us about how to build a great start-up and go "From Nil to Mil".

Greg Overholt

Founder of Students Offering Support


Greg Overholt is the founder and Executive Director at Students Offering Support (SOS), a non-profit organization that has spread to post-secondary and secondary institutions all over North America. He originally planned a career in the tech business world, but his passions have led him to a career in social entrepreneurship. Greg is a recipient of Waterloo's Top 40 Under 40 and has twice been a finalist for the Ernst & Young's Young Entrepreneur Award.
